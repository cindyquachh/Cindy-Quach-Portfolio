School Active Travel Program

During the fall semester of 2021, I enrolled in IAT 235. This course introduces theory and practice of designing visual representations of information. I learned to visually translate textual, numerical and evidentiary information so that it can be communicated to diverse user communities and contexts. The final project allowed students to incorporate their design thinking and coding skills to create a user friendly website for the Vancouver School Board. The entirety of the process from researching to designing to coding a functional website took roughly about one month.

Before starting any of the design processes, our team needed to comprehend the website’s purpose. We also needed to do our own investigation, certainly in close collaboration with the client, which in this case is the Vancouver School Board and the users. We sat down and considered these questions:

- Who is the site for?
- What exactly does the audience expect or look for?
- Is this website’s primary aim to inform, sell, or amuse?
- How do you want to stand out from the competitor sites?
- Are there any websites with designs that you like? Why?

UX Research: User Interviews (gain insight on how they feel towards the website)

UX Research: Identify User Pain Points (to involve identifying and understanding the challenges and frustrations that users face while interacting with a product or service)

UX Research: User Journey Mapping (helping UX designers understand the user’s experience and identify pain points and opportunities for improvement)

Problems our team encountered during the process were designing the overall layout of the website and coding it. Our team went through multiple designs and figured out which one worked well and aligned with the Vancouver School Board’s user’s needs and goals. After going through many iterations, we ultimately went forward with the design above. It showcases the information in a clean and simple layout with easy to see buttons and makes it easy for parents or other adults to look at.

The success of a project is often measured by the feedback received from those involved, and in this case, it seems that the efforts put in have paid off. The project has exceeded expectations, as it has received positive feedback from both peers and the teaching team. This feedback likely reflects the quality of the work produced, as well as the level of collaboration and communication demonstrated throughout the project. This project is a reflection of our hard efforts and showcases our talents as a UI designer.
